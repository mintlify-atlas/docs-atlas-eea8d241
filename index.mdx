Say `/nothing-design` and Claude builds interfaces that are monochromatic, typographically driven, and information-dense without clutter.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the skill">
    Clone the repo and copy the `nothing-design` folder into `~/.claude/skills/`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trigger it">
    In Claude Code, say `/nothing-design` or "Nothing style" to activate the skill.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your mode">
    Tell Claude whether you want dark mode (OLED black) or light mode (warm off-white). Both are first-class.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get your UI">
    Claude generates production-ready HTML/CSS, SwiftUI, or React/Tailwind following the Nothing design language.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What makes Nothing design different

The Nothing design language is built on a few hard rules that most design systems ignore:

- **Three layers only.** Every screen has exactly one primary element, supporting context, and metadata. Not two layers, not five.
- **Type is the hierarchy.** Scale, weight, and spacing communicate importance — not color, not icons, not borders.
- **Monochrome by default.** Color is an event. Red (`#D71921`) signals urgency. Everything else is grayscale.
- **Both modes are designed, not derived.** Dark mode is an instrument panel in a dark room. Light mode is a printed technical manual. Neither is an afterthought.

<Note>
  The skill will never trigger automatically for generic UI tasks. You must explicitly say "Nothing style", "Nothing design", or `/nothing-design` to activate it.
</Note>
